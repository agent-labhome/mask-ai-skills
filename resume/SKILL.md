## Overview
A resume is a strategic marketing document that summarizes your professional qualifications, skills, and achievements to secure interviews. Unlike a CV (which is comprehensive), a resume is typically 1-2 pages and tailored to specific opportunities.

## Core Principles

### 1. **Purpose-Driven**
Every element should answer \"Why should this employer interview me?\" 
- Focus on relevance over completeness
- Prioritize achievements over responsibilities
- Quantify impact whenever possible

### 2. **Audience-Aware**
- **Human Readers**: Clear, scannable, compelling narrative
- **ATS Systems**: Keyword-optimized, standard formatting, machine-readable
- **Hiring Managers**: Problem-solution framing, cultural fit indicators

### 3. **Value-Oriented**
Frame experience through the lens of value delivered:
- \"Increased sales by 30%\" not \"Responsible for sales\"
- \"Reduced processing time by 25%\" not \"Handled processing\"

## Practical Guide

### Phase 1: Foundation Building (2-3 hours)

#### A. Gather Raw Materials
1. **Complete Career Inventory**:
   - List every position (paid and unpaid)
   - Document all projects, achievements, metrics
   - Collect performance reviews, awards, recognitions
   - Note technologies, tools, methodologies used

2. **Research Target Roles**:
   - Analyze 5-10 job descriptions for your target position
   - Identify recurring keywords, requirements, and pain points
   - Note industry-specific terminology

#### B. Create Master Resume
- Include EVERYTHING (this document is for your eyes only)
- Use consistent formatting for easy copying/pasting
- Store in cloud storage for accessibility

### Phase 2: Structural Design (3-4 hours)

#### A. Choose Appropriate Format
- **Chronological**: Traditional, emphasizes career progression (best for linear careers)
- **Functional**: Skills-focused, minimizes employment gaps
- **Hybrid**: Combines both, most effective for modern job searches
