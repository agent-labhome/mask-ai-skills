## Overview
Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) refers to a business model where companies integrate social and environmental concerns into their operations and interactions with stakeholders. CSR moves beyond compliance to embrace responsibilities that contribute to sustainable development while addressing stakeholder expectations.

## Core Components of CSR

### 1. **Environmental Stewardship**
- **Climate Action:** Carbon footprint reduction, renewable energy adoption
- **Resource Management:** Water conservation, waste reduction, circular economy practices
- **Biodiversity Protection:** Sustainable land use, habitat preservation
- **Supply Chain Sustainability:** Green procurement, sustainable logistics

### 2. **Social Responsibility**
- **Employee Well-being:** Fair wages, safe working conditions, diversity & inclusion
- **Community Engagement:** Local development programs, philanthropy, volunteerism
- **Human Rights:** Ethical labor practices, supply chain transparency
- **Consumer Protection:** Product safety, responsible marketing, data privacy

### 3. **Economic Responsibility**
- **Ethical Governance:** Anti-corruption measures, transparent reporting
- **Sustainable Growth:** Long-term value creation, responsible innovation
- **Stakeholder Returns:** Fair shareholder practices, community reinvestment
- **Market Ethics:** Fair competition, responsible pricing

### 4. **Governance & Ethics**
- **Board Oversight:** CSR committee establishment, executive accountability
- **Transparency:** Regular CSR reporting, third-party verification
- **Ethical Culture:** Code of conduct, whistleblower protection
- **Risk Management:** ESG risk integration, crisis preparedness
