- **Domain**: Audio Production, Digital Media, Content Creation

---

## Practical Guide to Podcasting

### Phase 1: Foundation & Planning

#### 1.1 Concept Development
- **Niche Selection**: Identify a specific topic where you can provide unique value
- **Target Audience**: Define your ideal listener (demographics, interests, pain points)
- **Format Decisions**:
  - Solo commentary
  - Interview-based
  - Co-hosted conversations
  - Narrative/storytelling
  - Panel discussions
- **Episode Structure**: Standardize opening, main content, closing segments
- **Show Identity**:
  - Memorable name (check availability across platforms)
  - Clear value proposition
  - Consistent branding (cover art, tone, music)

#### 1.2 Technical Setup
**Essential Equipment:**
- **Microphone** (USB or XLR):
  - Entry: Blue Yeti, Audio-Technica ATR2100x ($100-150)
  - Mid-range: Rode PodMic, Shure MV7 ($200-300)
  - Professional: Shure SM7B, Electro-Voice RE20 ($400-500)
- **Headphones**: Closed-back for monitoring (Audio-Technica M20x, Sony MDR-7506)
- **Audio Interface** (for XLR mics): Focusrite Scarlett 2i2, Universal Audio Volt
- **Acoustic Treatment**: Portable vocal booth, foam panels, or record in soft-furnished rooms

**Software Stack:**
- **Recording**: Audacity (free), Adobe Audition, Hindenburg Journalist, Riverside.fm
- **Planning**: Notion, Airtable, or Google Docs for episode planning
- **Communication**: Zoom, SquadCast, or Riverside for remote interviews
