## Overview
Color grading is the process of enhancing and altering the color of visual media to achieve a specific aesthetic, emotional tone, or technical correction. Unlike color correction (which fixes issues), grading is a creative choice that defines visual style.

## Core Concepts

### 1. **Color Theory Fundamentals**
- **Color Wheel Relationships**: Complementary, analogous, triadic colors
- **Psychological Impact**: How colors evoke emotions (blue = calm, red = passion, etc.)
- **Color Harmony**: Creating visually pleasing combinations
- **Contrast & Saturation**: Using these to direct viewer attention

### 2. **Technical Foundations**
- **Bit Depth**: 8-bit vs. 10-bit vs. 12-bit color
- **Color Spaces**: sRGB, Rec.709, DCI-P3, Rec.2020
- **Gamma Curves**: Understanding log, linear, and display-referred workflows
- **Scopes**: Waveform, vectorscope, histogram, parade scope

### 3. **The Grading Pipeline**
1. **Technical Correction** (fixing exposure, white balance)
2. **Primary Grading** (global adjustments to entire image)
3. **Secondary Grading** (localized adjustments to specific areas/colors)
4. **Creative Look Development** (stylistic choices)
5. **Final Output** (matching delivery specifications)
