## What is Sound Design?

Sound design is the art and practice of creating, recording, manipulating, and integrating audio elements to support storytelling, enhance emotional impact, and build immersive environments across various media. It encompasses everything from subtle ambient textures to dramatic sound effects, from UI sounds in apps to creature vocals in films.

## Core Principles

### 1. **Emotional Architecture**
Sound directly influences emotion. Low frequencies often convey threat or power, while high frequencies can signal tension or fragility. The relationship between sound and visual elements creates meaning.

### 2. **Sonic Perspective**
Sounds exist in space. Design for proximity (close/far), environment (interior/exterior), and point of view (subjective/objective). Reverb, filtering, and volume create spatial relationships.

### 3. **Selective Realism**
Real-world sounds are often insufficient or uninteresting. Sound design frequently involves enhancing reality or creating entirely new sonic realities that feel \"right\" rather than \"real.\"

### 4. **Rhythm and Pacing**
Sound has tempo. The rhythm of effects, dialogue, and silence controls narrative pacing and audience attention.
